Korean cuisine

About korean food


Korean cuisine is largely based on rice, vegetables, seafood and (at least in South Korea),
meats. Dairy is largely absent from the traditional Korean diet. Korean
Foods are regulated by Korean cultural etiquette.

Recipes

Gimbap (김밥), also romanized as kimbap, is a Korean dish made from cooked rice and ingredients such as vegetables, fish and meats that are rolled in gim—dried sheets of seaweed—and served in bite-sized slices.

Sushi

  • 3 large eggs
  • Vegetable oil, for greasing the pan

  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 3 sheets flat odeng fishcake, cut lengthwise into 1/4-inch strips
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 3 long strips of artificial crab meat

Bulgogi

Korean Beef Bulgogi is a super easy way to enjoy tasty Korean food at home. A quick and simple marinade for flank steak is all that's needed to enjoy this delicious dish.

Natto
  • 2 pounds flank steak
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar firmly packed
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic minced or crushed
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 2 teaspoons oyster sauce
  • 1/2 cup sliced green onion

Eomuk

Eomuk (어묵) is processed seafood made with pureed fish. It translates to fish cake, but obviously not the fish cake known in Western cooking.

Eomuk
  • ½ pound fresh white fresh fish fillet
  • ¼ pound squid: cleaned, rinsed, and chopped
  • ¼ pound shrimp: shelled, deveined, rinsed, and drained
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • ½ medium onion
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 3 cups plus 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • ¼ cup all purpose flour
  • ¼ cup potato starch
  • 1 large egg white

Tteokpokki

Tteokbokki, 떡볶이, is a beloved Korean rice cake dish with many variations and a rich history.

Tteokpokki
  • 1 pound of cylinder shaped rice cake, bought or homemade
  • 4 cups of water
  • 7 large size dried anchovies, with heads and intestines removed
  • 6 x 8 inch dried kelp
  • ⅓ cup hot pepper paste
  • 1 tablespoon Korean hot pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 3 green onions, cut into 3 inch long pieces
  • 2 hard boiled eggs, shelled
  • ½ pound fish cakes

Pigodi

Pigodi are a type of Korean pies that are steamed. The most popular type is with meat and cabbage.

Pigodi
  • 450 g warm water
  • 2 tsp dry yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 800 g flour
  • 50 ml vegetable oil
  • pinch of salt
  • 300 g cabbage
  • 1.5 tsp salt
  • 350 g pork
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • pinch cayenne pepper
